I received my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from The University of Texas at Austin advised by Hovav Shacham. My dissertation focused on securing media parsing code, where I found vulnerabilities in H.264 decoders, and improved the performance of WASM sandboxed media libraries.

I was also a Strauss Center Brumley Next Generation Fellow mentored by Bobby Chesney, working on applications of zero-knowledge proofs to national security.

I was an M.Eng. student at the MIT Media Lab's Digital Currency Initiative advised by Neha Narula. My thesis was on the privacy and auditability of distributed ledgers, and part of it was later published at NSDI. My M.Eng. thesis is available here.

I also did my undergrad at MIT where I lived in Spanish House and had research opportunities on combining attribute-based encryption with proof-of-work under Shafi Goldwasser, and on improving SMT solver performance with program synthesis under Armando Solar-Lezama.

I have worked full time at Raytheon BBN Technologies, and interned at Trail of Bits (Winter '23), Cirrus Logic (Fall '21), Samsung Austin R&D Center (SARC) (Summer '19), Microsoft Research (Summer '18), Symantec (Summers '13 and '14), Secunetics (IAP '14), and Lockheed Martin (Summer '12).
